    # Instagram Reels Algorithm Knowledge Base

## Core Ranking Mechanics

### Recommendation Funnel

Instagram Reels are distributed through a multi-stage recommendation funnel.

Process:

1. A Reel is shown to an initial test audience of approximately 100–500 users.
2. The audience is selected based on predicted engagement probability.
3. Real-time engagement signals determine whether distribution expands or contracts.
4. Strong signals increase reach.
5. Weak signals suppress future impressions.

Primary objective:

Maximize user retention and total platform session time.

---

## Primary Ranking Factors

### Watch Time

Definition:
Total viewing duration accumulated by viewers.

Importance:
Very High

Algorithm Interpretation:
Longer watch duration indicates stronger audience retention.

Optimization Goals:
- Increase average watch duration.
- Reduce early exits.
- Maintain viewer attention throughout the Reel.

---

### Completion Rate

Definition:
Percentage of viewers who watch the Reel until the end.

Importance:
Very High

Algorithm Interpretation:
The content successfully delivered value or satisfied viewer curiosity.

Success Indicators:
- Above 70% completion rate.

Failure Indicators:
- Below 30% completion rate.

---

### Rewatches

Definition:
Number of times viewers replay the Reel.

Importance:
High

Algorithm Interpretation:
Can indicate strong interest.

Warning:
High rewatches combined with low completion may indicate confusion or poor pacing.

Positive Scenario:
- High completion rate.
- High rewatch rate.

Negative Scenario:
- Low completion rate.
- High rewatch rate.

---

### Shares

Definition:
Number of users sending the Reel through direct messages or stories.

Importance:
Highest

Algorithm Interpretation:
Represents trust transfer and viral distribution potential.

Success Threshold:
Above 2% of reach.

Failure Threshold:
Below 0.2% of reach.

---

### Saves

Definition:
Number of users bookmarking the Reel.

Importance:
High

Algorithm Interpretation:
Content provides long-term value.

Common Save Triggers:
- Educational content.
- Tutorials.
- Checklists.
- Reference material.

Success Threshold:
- Educational content: Above 1.5% of reach.
- Entertainment content: Above 0.5% of reach.

---

### Profile Visits

Definition:
Users visit the creator profile after viewing a Reel.

Importance:
Medium-High

Algorithm Interpretation:
Interest in consuming additional content from the creator.

---

### Follows

Definition:
Users follow the creator after watching a Reel.

Importance:
Medium-High

Algorithm Interpretation:
Strong audience conversion signal.

Success Threshold:
Above 0.5% of non-follower views.

---

### Comments

Definition:
Viewer-generated responses to a Reel.

Importance:
Medium

Algorithm Interpretation:
Community engagement signal.

Additional Value:
Reply chains increase engagement quality.

---

### Likes

Definition:
Users heart or double-tap a Reel.

Importance:
Low-Medium

Algorithm Interpretation:
Positive signal but weaker than retention, shares, and saves.

---

### Audience Interactions

Definition:
Additional audience participation signals.

Examples:
- Mentions
- Tags
- Poll responses
- Interactive story actions

Importance:
Medium

Algorithm Interpretation:
Indicates community activation.

---

## Secondary Ranking Factors

### Audio Usage

Definition:
Use of trending or original audio.

Purpose:
Helps categorize content and identify audience interests.

---

### Caption Text

Definition:
Text associated with the Reel.

Purpose:
Provides contextual information for categorization.

Importance:
Low

---

### Hashtags

Definition:
Keywords attached to a Reel.

Purpose:
Initial audience targeting and categorization.

Importance:
Low

Recommended Usage:
3–5 relevant hashtags.

Avoid:
- #fyp
- #viral
- #explorepage

---

### Account Authority

Definition:
Historical trust and performance score of an account.

Factors:
- Posting consistency
- Previous performance
- Community guideline history

---

### Session Context

Definition:
Viewer behavioral context during a session.

Examples:
- Time of day
- Typical usage habits
- Session length

Purpose:
Improve recommendation relevance.

## Exploration vs Exploitation

### Exploration

Purpose:
Discover emerging content.

Typical Share:
20–30% of impressions.

Content Types:
- Small creators
- New topics
- New audio

---

### Exploitation

Purpose:
Maximize engagement probability.

Typical Share:
70–80% of impressions.

Content Types:
- Proven creators
- High-retention content
- Familiar interests

## Audience Retention Principles

### Ideal Retention Curve

Characteristics:

- Above 70% retention after 2 seconds.
- Gradual decline.
- 60–70% completion.

---

### Retention Warning Signs

Condition:
More than 50% audience loss within first 3 seconds.

Interpretation:
Hook failure.

---

### Mid-Video Recovery

Definition:
Retention recovery after a secondary hook.

Methods:
- Scene changes
- Text overlays
- Audio changes

---

## Looping Behavior

### Positive Loop

Condition:
Viewer watches twice and reaches near completion again.

Interpretation:
Strong interest.

---

### Negative Loop

Condition:
Multiple automatic loops without completion.

Interpretation:
Unclear ending or confusing structure.

Potential Consequence:
Reduced distribution.

---

## High Performing Reel Characteristics

Requirements:

- Strong hook within 1–2 seconds.
- Completion rate above 70%.
- Watch time above 80% of duration.
- Rewatch rate between 10–25%.
- Share rate above 2%.
- Save rate above recommended thresholds.
- Follow conversion above 0.5%.
- Clear content structure.
- Native mobile aesthetic.
- Effective text overlays.

Structure:

Hook → Value Delivery → Payoff → CTA

---

## Low Performing Reel Characteristics

Indicators:

- Completion rate below 30%.
- Watch time below 40% of duration.
- Early drop-off above 50%.
- Share rate below 0.2%.
- Save rate below 0.1%.
- Confusing loops.
- Irrelevant hashtags.
- External watermarks.
- Low-quality visuals.
- Silent inactive sections.
- Excessive CTAs.

### Performance Classification Rules

#### Hook Failure

Condition:
Completion rate below 40%.

Primary Issue:
Weak opening.

---

#### End Drop-Off

Condition:
Completion rate between 40% and 60%.

Additional Condition:
Watch time above 70% of duration.

Primary Issue:
Viewers leave near the ending.

---

#### Confusing Loop

Condition:
Rewatch rate above 15%.

Additional Condition:
Completion rate below 50%.

Primary Issue:
Viewers replay without understanding content flow.

---

#### Underperformer

Condition:
Below 20th percentile in at least two primary metrics.

Primary Metrics:
- Watch time
- Completion rate
- Shares

---

#### Strong Retainer

Condition:
Completion rate above 70%.

Primary Issue:
None.

---

#### Viral Seed

Condition:
Strong retention combined with strong sharing behavior.

---

## Derived Metrics

### Share-To-View Ratio

Formula:

STV = Shares / Reach

Interpretation:

Below 0.5%:
Low virality.

---

### Save-To-View Ratio

Formula:

SAV = Saves / Reach

Interpretation:

Below 0.3%:
Low utility.

---

### Follow Conversion Ratio

Formula:

Follows / Non-Follower Views

Interpretation:

Below 0.2%:
Weak audience conversion.

## Recommendation Framework

### Hook Failure Recommendations

Problem:
Completion rate below 40%.

Actions:
- Rewrite first 3 seconds.
- Add visual interruption.
- Add stronger text overlays.
- Start with outcome before explanation.

---

### End Drop-Off Recommendations

Problem:
Viewers leave near the ending.

Actions:
- Move payoff earlier.
- Shorten ending.
- Reduce filler content.

---

### Confusing Loop Recommendations

Problem:
High rewatches and low completion.

Actions:
- Add explicit ending.
- Fade to black.
- Display END indicator.

---

### Low Share Recommendations

Problem:
Share rate below 0.5%.

Actions:
- Add relatable moments.
- Add surprising facts.
- Add sharing prompts.

---

### Low Save Recommendations

Problem:
Save rate below 0.3%.

Actions:
- Add step-by-step content.
- Add numbered frameworks.
- Add future reference value.

---

### Watermark Recommendations

Problem:
External branding reduces reach.

Actions:
- Export clean video.
- Remove platform watermarks.
- Use Instagram-native assets.

---

## Core Algorithm Decision Logic

Primary Question:

"Will showing this Reel increase the user's time on platform?"

Optimization Priority:

1. Watch Time
2. Completion Rate
3. Shares
4. Saves
5. Profile Visits
6. Follows
7. Comments
8. Likes

Rule:

When engagement metrics conflict, retention metrics win.

Example:

High Likes + Low Completion Rate
→ Reduced Distribution

High Completion Rate + High Shares
→ Increased Distribution
